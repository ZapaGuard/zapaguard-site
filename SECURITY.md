# Security Policy

ZapaGuard takes the security of its software and services seriously. We appreciate responsible disclosure of vulnerabilities and will work with you to resolve issues promptly.

## Reporting a Vulnerability

**Please do not report security vulnerabilities through public GitHub issues, discussions, or pull requests.**

Instead, report them privately via GitHub's private vulnerability reporting:

1. Go to the [Security tab](https://github.com/ZapaGuard/zapaguard-site/security) of this repository.
2. Click **Report a vulnerability**.
3. Fill in the advisory form with as much detail as possible.

Please include, where applicable:

- The type of issue (e.g. XSS, SSRF, authentication bypass, information disclosure)
- Affected URLs, endpoints, or source files
- Step-by-step instructions to reproduce the issue
- Proof-of-concept code, if available
- The impact you believe the issue has

## What to Expect

- We will acknowledge your report within **5 business days**.
- We will keep you informed of our progress as we investigate and remediate.
- We practice coordinated disclosure: please give us a reasonable opportunity to fix the issue before any public disclosure. We will credit reporters in the advisory unless you prefer to remain anonymous.

## Scope

This policy covers the code in this repository and the ZapaGuard website it powers. Vulnerabilities in third-party dependencies should be reported upstream; feel free to notify us as well if they affect ZapaGuard.

## Safe Harbor

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who:

- Act in good faith and within the scope above
- Avoid privacy violations, data destruction, and service disruption
- Do not access or modify data that does not belong to them
- Give us reasonable time to remediate before disclosing publicly

Thank you for helping keep ZapaGuard and its users safe.
